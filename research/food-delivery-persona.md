\## 4. Current Coping Mechanisms (No Insurance)



When disruptions occur, workers:

1\. \*\*Borrow from family/friends\*\* (most common)

2\. \*\*Take loans from moneylenders\*\* (15вАУ20% monthly interest)

3\. \*\*Reduce daily expenses\*\* (skip meals, delay rent)

4\. \*\*Work extra hours next week\*\* (burnout risk)

5\. \*\*Switch platforms temporarily\*\* (if one is down)

6\. \*\*Take side gigs\*\* (warehouse work, construction)



\*\*Problem:\*\* No structured safety net вЖТ Debt cycles

\## 7. Key Insights for GigGuard Design



\### What Workers Need

1\.  \*\*Affordable:\*\* вВє50вАУ80/week is acceptable (1вАУ2 orders worth)

2\.  \*\*Simple onboarding:\*\* 2вАУ3 minute signup max

3\.  \*\*No paperwork:\*\* Hate filling forms

4\.  \*\*Instant payout:\*\* Within minutes, not days

5\.  \*\*Clear triggers:\*\* "If rain >50mm, you get вВє400" (simple rule)

6\.  \*\*Weekly renewal:\*\* Matches their cash flow



\### What Won't Work

1\.  Monthly premiums (too long a commitment)

2\.  Complex claim forms (they'll abandon)

3\.  Payouts after 2 weeks (need money NOW)

4\.  Unclear triggers (subjective claims)

5\.  Expensive (>вВє100/week = too high)
